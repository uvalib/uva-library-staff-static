Organizational Design

About the New Organizational Design

  • Decisions about the Library's new organizational design and implementation were informed by 8 Organizational Principles.
  • The design includes five distinct areas, each of which is led by a team of staff (a lead and co-leads) who collaborate to help develop each area.
  • Roles and and accountabilities for the University Librarian, Senior Leadership Team, and Extended Leadership Team have been drafted and are now available.

Organizational Areas

Each organizational area has a lead, and all leads constitute the Senior Leadership Team (SLT). Martha Sites, University Librarian and Dean of Libraries, is also a member of the SLT. SLT members along with co-leads for each area make up the Extended Leadership Team (ELT).

Academic Engagement

To add significant value to and promote excellence in research, scholarship, artistic production, teaching, and learning for faculty, students, and staff at the University of Virginia and across the broader academic community.

Administration & Planning

To provide responsive and reliable administrative services for the Library, and help all parts of the organization achieve strategic goals while adhering to University policies and guidelines.

Collections Access & Discovery

To create University of Virginia Library collections development and management strategies for all types of materials in traditional and emerging formats, in order to guide decision making and to support diverse learning and research environments for University students, faculty, and staff, now and in the future.

Learn More

You are welcomed and encouraged to contact leadership with your questions and concerns to learn more about the organizational design and process. There are many ways to communicate with the SLT/ELT and colleagues:
  • Weekly and Monthly Coffees
    • These open Q&A sessions take place weekly on Fridays at 9:30am in the designated location, and once a month at the designated time and location.
  • Leadership Blog
    • SLT and ELT members post their thoughts and news of interest on the Library Leadership Notes blog. You may subscribe to the blog to receive email notifications. Comments are welcomed.
  • Email and Appointments
    • Please feel free to arrange meetings with and/or email individual leaders as you'd like.
